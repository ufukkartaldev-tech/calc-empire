# Ohm's Law

## Definition

Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance between them.

## Basic Formula

$$V = I \times R$$

Where:

- **V** = Voltage (volts, V)
- **I** = Current (amperes, A)
- **R** = Resistance (ohms, Ω)

## Example Calculation

### Given:

- Voltage (V) = 12 V
- Resistance (R) = 4 Ω

### Find:

Current (I)

### Solution:

$$I = \frac{V}{R} = \frac{12}{4} = 3 \text{ A}$$

## Practical Applications

- Circuit design and analysis
- Component selection
- Power supply sizing
- Fault diagnosis

## Safety Considerations

- Always verify calculations with a multimeter
- Consider power ratings (P = V × I)
- Use appropriate safety margins
- Follow electrical codes and standards
